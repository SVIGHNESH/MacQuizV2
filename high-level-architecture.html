How MacQuiz works,
explained for everyone

No jargon required. This page shows the whole system in one picture: who uses it, the four layers it is built from, what happens on quiz day, and the rules the system is never allowed to break.

The people

Three kinds of users, three jobs

Nobody signs themselves up. One admin creates every account, which is exactly what makes the platform safe to run real exams on.

The Admin

Runs the institution's side of things. Creates every teacher and student account, organizes students into class groups, and can see activity across the whole organization. The admin never writes quizzes, so responsibility stays clear.

The Teacher

Owns the quiz from start to finish: writes the questions (or uploads a spreadsheet of them), picks exactly which students may see it, sets the date and time, watches everyone live while it runs, and reviews the results afterwards.

The Student

Sees only the quizzes assigned to them, and can start only during the scheduled window. Their answers are saved automatically every few seconds, so a crashed laptop or dropped Wi-Fi never loses work.

The big picture

Four layers, one server

Everything a user does travels down through four layers and the answer travels back up. Each layer has one clear job.

1 · The appswhat people see

One website with three faces. It shows each person only what their role allows.

Admin console
Add people, manage groups, see the whole organization.
Teacher app
Write, schedule, watch live, analyze.
Student app
See your quizzes, take them, view your results.
every click travels over a secure connection
2 · The front doorchecks & directs

Before any request reaches the server, it passes a checkpoint.

Gatekeeper (API gateway)
Confirms who you are and whether you are allowed to do what you are asking. Blocks anything suspicious or too frequent.
Like the receptionist who checks your ID before letting you into the building.
Live line (realtime gateway)
Keeps an open connection to every active user so updates arrive instantly, without anyone refreshing the page.
Like an intercom that stays on during the exam instead of passing notes.
approved requests go to the brain
3 · The brainwhere decisions happen

One program, organized into focused departments. Because it is one program on one machine, it is simple to run and costs almost nothing to host.

Accounts
Logins, passwords, and the single rulebook of who may do what.
Quizzes
Questions, schedules, and who is invited. Freezes a quiz the moment it is published so it cannot change mid-exam.
Attempts
The exam invigilator. Keeps official time, saves every answer, ends attempts exactly on the deadline, and enforces a teacher's decision to remove someone.
Analytics
Turns raw results into scores, charts, and question difficulty after the quiz closes.
Background helpers
Do the slow chores off to the side: checking uploaded question files, grading, opening and closing quizzes on schedule.
everything important is written down
4 · The memorywhere everything lives

Four kinds of storage, each chosen for what it is best at.

The record book (PostgreSQL)
The permanent, authoritative copy of everything: people, quizzes, every saved answer, every action taken.
The school's official register. If it is not written here, it did not happen.
The whiteboard (Redis)
Fast, temporary notes: who is online right now and live updates in flight.
Quick notes that make things fast; nothing precious lives only here.
The store room (file storage)
Uploaded spreadsheets, question images, exported reports, and nightly backup copies.
The to-do list (job queue)
Slow tasks wait in line here so the app stays fast even when 2,000 students finish at once.
Why this shape

A quiz platform is idle most days, then intensely busy for one hour. One well-organized server handles that hour with roughly ten times the needed capacity, which is why the whole thing runs on a free-tier machine (see the deployment plan, DEP-001). If the school ever grows past it, each department is already built so it can move to its own machine without a rewrite.

A day in the life

What actually happens on quiz day

Days before
The teacher prepares

Questions are written one by one or uploaded as a spreadsheet, which the system checks row by row before accepting all of it or none of it. The teacher picks the students, sets Friday 10:00-11:00 with 30 minutes per student, and publishes. Publishing takes a frozen snapshot of the quiz, so nothing can change under the students later.

Friday 10:00:00
The quiz opens itself

A scheduled task flips the quiz to live at the exact second, using the server's clock. No one has to press a button, and a student's laptop clock being wrong changes nothing.

10:00-10:01
A thousand students start at once

This stampede is the moment the system is designed around. The frozen quiz is kept ready on the whiteboard, so handing it to each student is nearly free, and every answer is saved to the record book within seconds of being chosen.

While it runs
The teacher watches live

The live dashboard shows every student's row: which question they are on, how many they have answered, who lost connection. If a student leaves fullscreen or switches tabs, the row quietly gains an amber note, evidence for the teacher, not an automatic punishment.

If needed
The teacher can remove someone

Removal takes two steps and a written reason. The moment it is confirmed, the server stops accepting anything from that attempt, no matter what the student's device does. The work done so far is kept and graded, so any dispute can be settled from the record.

Each deadline
Time runs out safely

Each student's attempt ends at their own deadline, even if they closed the laptop and walked away, whatever was already saved is submitted automatically. At 11:00 the quiz closes for everyone.

Minutes after
Grading and results

The helpers grade every attempt from the frozen answer key, then build the analytics: score distribution, hardest questions, each student's progress over time. Teachers see their classes; the admin sees the whole institution.

The guarantees

Five rules the system never breaks

The server's clock is the only clock

Quizzes open, close, and time out on server time. The countdown on a student's screen is just a courtesy display of the real one.

Answer keys never leave the building

Correct answers are stripped out of everything sent to a student, checked at one central point so no future feature can leak them by accident.

Saved work survives anything

Answers are saved as they are chosen. A crash, a dead battery, or a dropped connection costs a student nothing; they resume exactly where they were.

Only invited students know a quiz exists

An unassigned student cannot see, open, or even confirm the existence of a quiz that is not theirs.

Everything important leaves a paper trail

Every account created, quiz published, and student removed is written to a permanent log with who, what, when, and why. Nothing is silently deleted.
